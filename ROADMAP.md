

# Design considerations
	
More details on my thoughts are available on the [development blog](https://averagebear.game.blog/):
- Benevolent Dictator veto:
	1. Permadeath, no exceptions
	2. No Phoenix/Casual mode
	3. No Ambush spawns
	4. No Teleportation
 	5. No Time travel
	6. No Dragons
	7. Minimal so-called "fanservice"
	8. No Digital Rights Management (DRM) software

Final decision, no discussion. 
If you want a game with these features, go buy FE Fates or make your own. 

- Linear, chapter-based
	- Army movement on the world map only if it makes narrative sense
	- Certain story Events unlockable on world-map
	- Side chapters unlockable.
	- Characters hint at secrets, army scribe/assistant take notes in books
	- Enemy encounters on world Map are rare
	- Camp between chapters where you can move around on the map and talk to people
 	- Cities visitable like camps

- Items
	- Breakable
	- Becomes unusable "broken trash" item when broken
	- Weapon's remaining number of uses can be increased using whetstones

- Units have two hands
	- Swords & shields
	- Dual Wielding
	- Handedness/Ambidextry
	- Cavaliers need one hand on bridle
	- Anyone (almost) can ride a horse. 
	- No horse on promotion, get horses through gameplay. Ibid. for flying units. 

- Questionable features:
	- IronMan Mode
	Automatic game save + deletion of previous save after every move? After every chapter? Game Over deletes your savefile?

	- Forcible IronMan Mode
	Force player into IronMan mode. The game always saves, no retries. 

	- No Achievements
	I do not care for them and I think they are generally harmful.
